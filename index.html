About

I'm a statistician and statistical programmer — a PhD in Statistics & Data Science and roughly five years in SAS/CDISC clinical programming. My doctoral research developed a Bayesian zero-inflated, unipolar mixture item-response-theory model with covariate-dependent latent classification, applied to patient-reported symptom data (alcohol-use-disorder items from the NSDUH survey): Bayesian hierarchical modeling, latent-class structure, and questionnaire/PRO measurement. Over the last year I designed and built a "virtual biometrics department" — a system of AI agents that produce and independently QC SDTM/ADaM datasets and analysis documents for oncology trials.

Its guiding principle is "validate the wrappers, not the brain." The LLM is treated as a qualified author whose output can't leave the system until deterministic, independently checkable gates pass and a named human signs off — no autonomous submission, every applied rule citable and versioned. That posture is what lets modern AI touch a regulated, high-stakes domain at all, and it's the thread through all three projects above.
